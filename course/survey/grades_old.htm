Industrial Survey Grades

Explanations

The grades of the works submitted and checked so far  are available at the table below. The first two columns are general:

A - Absence One point was deducted for each missed lecture, which means two for each missed meeting.
B - Bonuses Total bonuses. Note that a 5% for the major lecture means 2.5 points for the final grade, since the major lecture is 50% of the entire course's grade.

The next five columns have values between 0 and 10, and together grade your major lecture:

T - Timing Using the full hour, arriving on time, splitting time between speakers, submitting the written parts on time.
P - Presentation The PowerPoint presentation: Logical order of things, clarity, use of graphical aids.
S - Speech Explaining the material well, attracting and controlling the audience, focusing on the key features of the technology.
K - Knowledge Deep understanding of the material, answering questions, level of depth and lecture.

E - Examples

 The quality, clarity and originality of the two demos.

The next two columns also have values between 0 and 10, and together grade your small subject (20% of the total grade):

C - Coverage Covering all important aspects well, evidence of thorough study, providing good links.

W - Writing

 Clarity, Logical order and depth of explanation, and the level of the demo.

The last three columns are also between 0 to 10,  and grade the project (30% of the total grade):

R - Range of Technologies Number of technologies and non-trivial issues learnt and used.

D - Difficulty

 Technical complexity of implementing the project, taking into account using beta software, unavailability of documentation or debuggers, enormous coding, etc.
U - Usability Documentation, clear explanations, installation instructions, references and anything else which will help others learn the technology starting from your project.

Final grades will be available once all three assignments have been submitted. Please mail David regarding possible errors in your 'Absence' or 'Bonus' grades.
